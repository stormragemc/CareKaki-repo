# AiMao × CareKaki — 5-Minute Live Demo Script

**Exactly 5 minutes, spoken as voiceover.** Stage directions in `[brackets]`. Each beat is tagged
with the **judging criterion** it targets. Speak warmly; slow down on the panda and the chat.

> **Criteria:** ① Problem Significance · ② Innovation · ③ Technical Quality · ④ Feasibility · ⑤ Presentation

---

## 0:00 – 0:40 · The loneliness nobody tracks  →  ①

`[Landing screen. Stand beside it. Warm, unhurried.]`

Singapore is ageing fast — and most seniors aren't in hospitals. They're at home, alone. They have children and grandchildren, but everyone's busy, everyone's far, and the calls get shorter.

`[Beat.]`

So a quiet thing happens: the family stops *noticing.* The skipped breakfasts, the new unsteadiness — the early signs of decline slip by, until one day it's an emergency. This is **hidden isolation.** Help exists; nobody's watching gently, every day.

Meet **AiMao** — a companion seniors actually *want* to talk to, that turns those daily chats into care a family can act on.

---

## 0:40 – 1:10 · One front door · meet AiMao  →  ② ⑤

`[Sign in as Mr Lim — lonely senior, daughter overseas. /home loads: the panda front and centre.]`

Mr Lim, seventy-two. Lives alone; his daughter's in London. And this is his home screen — no dashboard, no charts. Just AiMao, breathing, blinking: *"Good morning. How are you feeling today?"*

This is our whole idea: **AiMao is the face; CareKaki is the intelligence underneath.** A senior sees a friend. Behind the friend is a full care engine.

---

## 1:10 – 2:25 · The conversation — where care happens  →  ② ③ ⑤

`[Tap "Talk to AiMao". The panda sits atop the chat.]`

Watch the face. When Mr Lim speaks, it **listens.** When it's working something out, it **thinks.** For a lonely senior, that presence *is* the point.

`[Type/speak: "I didn't finish breakfast again, and I had to hold the table when I stood up."]`

No forms — he just talks. `[Point right.]` And a **Living Care Profile** assembles itself from the conversation. `[Point to the pulsing field.]` There — *mobility, needed support standing.* We never asked; AiMao noticed.

`[Turn on Audio Guide; tap the mic.]`

And AiMao **speaks back**, in a real human voice through ElevenLabs — a senior never has to type, they just talk.

`[MULTI-LANGUAGE BEAT — type in Bahasa Indonesia: "Pagi ini dia tidak mau makan."]`

And in a country like ours, this matters — AiMao meets people in **their own language.** English, Mandarin, Malay, Tamil, Bahasa Indonesia. `[Point as it replies in-language.]` No one left out because of language. `[Warm aside:]` Our whole team is Indonesian — so this one's personal.

---

## 2:25 – 3:10 · The Care Plan — clinical & printable  →  ① ④

`[Tap "View care plan" → /care-plan. Scroll.]`

From that chat, AiMao builds a real **care-plan document** — a plain-language summary, a week at a glance, and charts that answer real questions: *is he keeping it up? Is his steadiness improving?*

`[Open one activity card.]`

Every activity is spelled out like a clinician would — steps, the caregiver's role, and *stop and review if* dizziness or chest pain appears. Everything traces back to a fact about *him.*

`[Hit Print.]`

And it **prints**, clean, on A4 — for the fridge, or the polyclinic. It fits how families already work.

---

## 3:10 – 4:10 · AiMao's Mind — the engine  →  ③ ④

`[From Home, open the quiet "Explore how AiMao thinks" → AiMao's Mind.]`

For the builders in the room — this is the intelligence beneath the friendly face. A senior never sees it, but it's all real.

`[Point to the reasoning strip.]`

AiMao takes an observation, extracts signals, updates the profile, detects change against his own baseline, runs it through **Guardian**, and prepares the brief.

`[Sweep the live panels.]`

And these run on **real Singapore data** — nearby eldercare centres from a government GeoJSON on a live map; a medication safety check against the **HSA drug registry** and the US FDA's public labels; and a real **Telegram** message, on a real phone, that alerts the caregiver and hands the case to a Care Corner coordinator.

`[Point to the Guardian band.]`

Wrapping all of it — **Guardian.** No medical advice, ever. NRIC and personal data scrubbed before storage. And nothing irreversible runs until a human approves. Safety and privacy aren't a slide — they're a service in the code.

---

## 4:10 – 4:45 · Care Brief — closing the loop  →  ④ ①

`[Open the Care Brief.]`

And this is what a Care Corner coordinator receives — one clean page: what AiMao noticed, what was done, what to check next. Copy, share, or print in a tap.

So when the coordinator calls, they already know everything. Mr Lim — and his daughter in London — never repeat the story. The lonely senior stays *seen.*

---

## 4:45 – 5:00 · Close  →  ⑤

`[Back to Home — the panda, breathing.]`

Excellent care already exists in Singapore. AiMao is how a lonely senior *reaches* it — through a companion they look forward to, in their own language, every day.

**A face they trust. An engine that acts. A family that finally knows.**

That's AiMao. Thank you.

`[Stop. Let the panda blink.]`

## Presenter notes

- **Slow on the chat + panda** (Beats 3). That's where judges *feel* it. Everything else, keep moving.
- **The multi-language beat is genuinely demoable** — the chat runs on a multilingual LLM + ElevenLabs, so typing in Bahasa Indonesia really replies in-language (backend must be up). If it's offline, keep the line as *"AiMao is built to speak your language"* and move on — don't stall.
- **AiMao's Mind is your technical credibility.** Say the concrete words: *government GeoJSON, HSA registry, openFDA, Telegram, ElevenLabs, Guardian, approval gate.*
- **"Live or simulated?"** — the chat, profile, care plan, maps, drug lookups, Telegram and Guardian are real; agency *bookings* are simulated against real interfaces (a partnership step, not engineering).
- **Timing:** if short, cut the Print demo; if long, gesture at charts instead of describing them. On the last line — stop. Let the panda blink.
